Altitude acclimatization Important
Cortina sits at 1,224m. The trails reach 2,500m+. Don't ride hard the first day — headaches and fatigue are common if you jump straight to altitude. An easy afternoon walk is ideal.

MTB Day 1 — Cinque Torri
Cortina · Gondola up · 5 Towers trail · 1,800–2,360m
🚵 Full MTB Day 🏔️ 2,360m 🏨 Cortina
▾
🚵Main Ride — Cinque Torri Loop
📍 Cinque Torri
🏔️
Cinque Torri Loop eMTB Ideal
Take the Bai de Dones gondola up to ~2,000m, then ride the loop around the iconic Five Towers rock formations. WW1 trenches still visible on the mountain. Views of the Tofane and Lagazuoi massifs are unforgettable. ~18km, mostly intermediate terrain, perfect for teens.
💡 Take gondola up (saves 600m climb), ride down or loop. Gondola: ~20€/person. Start early — afternoon thunderstorms common in August.
⛅
Weather Warning August Rule
Start rides by 8–9am. Dolomite thunderstorms build fast from early afternoon. Be off exposed ridges by 1pm. If you see dark clouds building to the west, descend immediately.
🥩Lunch on the Mountain
🍽️
Rifugio Cinque Torri At 2,137m
📍 Rifugio Cinque Torri
Alpine hut right at the Five Towers. Schlutzkrapfen (spinach ravioli), goulash, apple strudel. Surreal setting — rock towers above, Dolomite panorama around. Cash only.
🛁Evening Recovery
🍦
Cortina Town Evening
📍 Corso Italia
Walk Corso Italia (the pedestrian main street). Gelato at Gelateria Artigianale, aperitivo at Bar Lovat. The town is lively in summer evenings — great atmosphere for the family.

🏞️Morning Stop — Lago di Braies
📍 Lago di Braies
🌊
Lago di Braies (Pragser Wildsee) Must Do
The most photographed lake in the Dolomites — emerald green water reflecting vertical limestone walls. Rowboats for rent. Arrive before 9am to beat the crowds and parking chaos. ~30 min detour off the Cortina → Val Gardena route.
💡 Seriously — arrive before 9am. After 10am the parking lot fills up and it's chaos. It's worth the early start.
🛣️Drive to Val Gardena
🛣️ 1.5h from Cortina (add 30 min for Lago di Braies stop). Route through Brunico, then SS242 into the valley. Last stretch into Ortisei is spectacular.
📍 Ortisei, Val Gardena
🚵Afternoon — Rent eMTBs + Easy Ride
🚵
Intersport Ortisei eMTB Rental Book Ahead
Largest selection in the valley. Full-suspension eMTB, sizes for adults and teens. Multi-day rentals available. Pick up bikes, do an easy afternoon loop to get oriented.
💡 Cost: ~60–80€/bike/day. Buy a Dolomiti Superski bike lift pass here too — covers all gondolas in the area for your riding days.

MTB Day 2 — Alpe di Siusi
Europe's largest high-altitude alpine meadow · Gondola up · 25km panorama trail
🚵 Full MTB Day 🏔️ 2,000m 🏨 Val Gardena
▾
🚵Main Ride — Alpe di Siusi Panorama Trail
📍 Alpe di Siusi
🏔️
Panorama Trail Loop ⭐ Best Day
Take the Ortisei gondola up to Alpe di Siusi (Seiser Alm) — the largest high-altitude alpine plateau in Europe. 25km of flowing singletrack through wildflower meadows with the Sassolungo and Sassopiatto massifs towering above. eMTB perfect here — long distances, moderate climbing. Accessible and spectacular for teens.
💡 This is the highlight of the whole trip. Take your time. The Bullaccia area is gentler for warm-up; Panorama Trail has more flow and views.
🚡
Ortisei Cable Car
Gondola from Ortisei center up to Alpe di Siusi. Bikes go in a separate gondola. Return by gondola or ride down (advanced). Covered by Dolomiti Superski bike pass.
🍽️Lunch — Rifugio on the Alm
🥾
Rifugio Compatsch or Rifugio Zallinger
📍 Rifugio Compatsch
Multiple rifugi (alpine huts) across the plateau. Speck und Knödel (bacon dumplings), Kaiserschmarrn (Austrian pancake dessert), local beer. Incredible terrace views. Book a table or arrive early.

Planning Notes
📌 Book Before You Go
🚗Rental car — Book SUV or wagon now. August rates spike. Pick up at FCO, drop in Verona.
🚵eMTBs — Reserve at Sport Varda (Cortina) and Intersport Ortisei (Val Gardena) online. August bikes go fast, especially teen sizes.
🍽️Restaurants — Book Osteria Le Logge (Siena) and a Val Gardena dinner spot. August walk-ins are a gamble.
🚄Train — Verona → Rome Frecciarossa. Book at trenitalia.com. Early booking = half the price.
🎫Dolomiti Superski Bike Pass — Covers gondolas across the whole area. Worth it for 2+ riding days. Buy in Ortisei.
⚠️ Things to Know
🇮🇹Ferragosto (Aug 16) — National holiday. Fuel stations, supermarkets, many restaurants closed. Stock up before leaving Rome.
⛈️Afternoon thunderstorms — Daily in August. Start rides by 8–9am, be off exposed ridges by 1pm. Non-negotiable.
🏔️Altitude — Trails reach 2,500m. First day light activity only. Headaches are common for kids if you push too hard too fast.
💶South Tyrol is expensive — This region (Alto Adige / Südtirol) is the most expensive in Italy. Budget 180–280€/night for accommodation, 25–35€/person for dinner.
🅿️Parking in Dolomites — Paid everywhere, 15–25€/day. Many mountain areas require ZTL pass or are car-free. Use gondolas and shuttles.
🚵 eMTB Tips for This Trip
🔋Battery range — Full day eMTB in the Dolomites uses 50–80% battery depending on mode. Eco mode extends range. Rifugi can sometimes charge your bike during lunch.
🪖Helmets — Full-face or enduro helmet recommended. Rentals include a helmet but you can bring your own if preferred.
👕Pack — Layers are essential. It can be 28°C at the bottom and 12°C at 2,500m. Rain jacket always in the pack.
📱Komoot app — Download the Dolomites MTB map pack before arriving. Works offline. Best navigation for trails in this region.
